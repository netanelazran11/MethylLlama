CpG Analysis · Slide 6 / 8
⚖️ Combined Informativeness Score — Variance + Age Correlation
Formula
score(CpG) = 0.5 × rank(variance) + 0.5 × rank(|age corr|)
Each CpG is ranked by two independent criteria:
  • Variance rank — computed on ALL 10,656 samples (unsupervised)
  • Correlation rank — computed on TRAIN only (prevents leakage)
Equal weighting (0.5 each) balances both signals.

Why Not Variance-Only Filter?
Problem: Known clock CpGs (e.g. ELOVL2 locus) show low population variance but high age-correlation because methylation changes are monotone and tissue-specific — they look "nearly constant" when you average across tissues.
Example: cg04528819
  Variance rank: bottom 10% (<var 0.001 threshold)
  |r| rank: top 0.1% (|r|=0.559)
A var-only filter at 0.005 discards it.
The combined score keeps it.
Why Not Correlation-Only Filter?
Problem: CpGs with very low variance have near-zero correlation by construction (denominator in Pearson r is ≈0). These near-constant CpGs would rank near the bottom in both metrics, so they're excluded by the combined score.
Benefit of variance rank: it captures CpGs that vary across tissues even when not directly age-predictive — tissue classification signal that still enriches the pooled representation.

V4b (next step): filter to top-10,000 CpGs by combined score. Expected: 1.33× more age signal per token, 50% shorter sequences (faster attention), less noise in mean pooling.
